CT chest with contrast

Search pattern

Confirm patient details and scan date

In particular review areas not covered on the CT images e.g. shoulders, neck, arms, and abdomen

  • Check that the ends and any side holes of tubes are appropriately positioned

    Follow airways from the trachea to the segmental and subsegmental level:
  • Are there any missing airways? - secondary to obstruction or prior resection
  • Any endobronchial lesions or airway debris?
  • Bronchial calibre
  • Bronchial wall thickness

  • Any pneumothorax?
  • Any pleural effusions? (check the density)
  • Any pleural nodules, masses, plaques, or calcification?

  • Check for large areas of consolidation, large masses, or ground glass opacities
  • Look for abnormal lucencies (emphysema, cysts, mosaic attenuation, honeycombing)
  • Look at the interstitium for septal thickening or reticulation
  • Look for smaller nodules and masses (both solid and ground glass)
  • Look for small nodules on MIPs

  • Look for fluid or air within the mediastinum
  • Examine the heart for size, filling defects, coronary calcification, pericardial effusion
  • Assess the vasculature: pulmonary arteries, pulmonary veins, aorta, aortic branches, SVC, IVC, sublavian and axilliary veins
  • Looks at the mediastinal lymph node stations: supraclavicular, axillary, mediastinal, hilar, subpectoral, internal thoracic
  • Oesophagus
  • Lower neck/thyroid

  • Any free/extraluminal gas?
  • Look at the liver, biliary tree, gallbladder, pancreas, stomach, bowel, spleen, adrenal glands, and imaged kidneys
  • Check imaged upper abdominal vasculature
  • Any ascites/free fluid?
  • Any enlarged lymph nodes?
  • Any peritoneal/omental deposits?

  • Focal bone lesions: benign vs aggressive
  • Fractures e.g. vertebral body heights
  • Musculature and subcutaneous tissues
  • Any breast masses or abnormal calcification?

Example normal report

Findings:

Normal appearances of the lung parenchyma.

No focal lung lesions.

No pleural thickening or pleural effusions.

The central airways are normal.

The heart, large vessels, and mediastinum are normal.

No enlarged supraclavicular or intrathoracic lymph nodes.

Normal appearanes of the imaged upper abdominal viscera.

No aggressive bone lesions.

Opinion:

No thoracic pathology.
