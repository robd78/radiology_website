Occasionally abnormal skull morphology, fractures and previous neurosurgical procedures are easier to appreciate on the scout images

Suggested window levels: W:350-400 HU L:20-60 HU Look for any subgaleal haematoma. Examine the globes, sinuses, palate, and pharnyx

Suggested window levels: W:2800 HU L: 600 HU Review the skull vault for any fractures or destructive bone lesions. Spend time reviewing the skull base due to the anatomical complexity. Ensure both TMJs are normally aligned.

Suggested window levels: W 80 HU L 180 HU. Review for any hyperdense collections. Pay particular attention to the tentorium and occiptal horns of the lateral ventricles.

Suggested window levels: W 80 HU L: 40 HU Review the basal cisterns and sulci. Are they visible and commensurate with ehe patient's age and ventricular size?Do they extend to the bone? If not is there any subacute (isodense) haemorrhage?Is there any subarachnoid blood? Look in the dependent areas particularly e.g. interpeduncular fossa and occipital horns of the lateral ventricles. Review the basilar tip and MCAs for any hyperdense vessel sign.

Suggested window levels: W 80 HU L: 40 HU Start at the vertex. Look at the brain matter and gyral pattern. Are there any masses, abnormal attenuation, or mass effect?

Suggested window levels: W: 30 HU L: 30 HU Search for subtle cortical hypoattenuation by assessing grey-white matter differentiation. The cortical and deep grey matter should be distinct from the white matter. Pay particular attenton to the insular cortex and basal ganglia.

Findings:

No acute intracranial haemorrhage or established large territory infarction. No intracranial mass on this non-contrast study. Normal configuration of the ventricles and basal cisterns. The calvarium and imaged skull base are intact. The imaged paranasal sinuses and mastoid air cells are clear.

Opinion:

No acute intracranial pathology
